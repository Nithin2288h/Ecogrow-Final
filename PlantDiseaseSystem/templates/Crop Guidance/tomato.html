🌱 Tomato Growing Guide

Week -4 to -3: Planning and Seed Starting

Choose climate-suited varieties like Arka Vikas or Pusa Ruby. Start seeds in trays indoors with organic potting mix. Keep warm and moist. Consider soil testing.

Week -2: Soil Preparation

Pick a sunny, well-drained spot. Clear weeds and rocks. Enrich soil with compost or vermicompost. Raised beds optional but helpful.

Week -1: Final Preparation

Level the soil and mark plant spacing. Harden off indoor seedlings gradually for outdoor adaptation.

Week 1: Transplanting

Transplant seedlings gently. Water deeply. Mulch with organic matter like straw or dried leaves.

Week 2: Establishment

Regular deep watering, avoid wet foliage. Hand-weed and check for pests.

Week 3: Supporting Vines

Stake indeterminate varieties. Continue weeding and watering. Optional foliar feed.

Week 4: Pruning and Monitoring

Prune suckers (indeterminate). Avoid pruning determinate types. Monitor closely for pests.

Week 5-6: Flowering

Water & nutrients crucial. Hand-pollinate if needed. Monitor and spray organic treatments.

Week 7-9: Fruit Set

Ensure consistent watering to avoid blossom-end rot. Prune suckers. Netting optional for fruit protection.

Week 10-12: Ripening

Reduce nitrogen, increase potassium. Observe ripening stages. Ensure sunlight exposure.

Harvest when fruits are fully colored and slightly soft. Pick gently to avoid bruising. Regular harvesting encourages new fruits. Compost old plants if disease-free.

  • Use heat-tolerant varieties suitable for Madurai.
  • Drip irrigation conserves water and improves consistency.
  • Rotate crops and companion plant with basil or marigold.
  • Use neem oil, insecticidal soap, and handpicking.
  • Ensure airflow, avoid overhead watering, and use organic fungicides wisely.
  • Feed soil regularly with compost for long-term health.
