🧅 Onion Growing Guide

A Step-by-Step Organic Cultivation Plan

Week -4 to -2: Seed Bed Preparation

Choose high-yielding local or CO-4 varieties. Prepare raised nursery beds with compost + Trichoderma. Sow seeds after soaking in panchagavya for 6 hrs.

Week -2 to 0: Main Field Preparation

Plough field 2–3 times. Add 10 tons FYM/acre + 200 kg neem cake. Add Azospirillum, Phosphobacteria biofertilizers. Form ridges & furrows at 30 cm apart.

Week 1: Transplanting

Transplant 40-day-old seedlings (8–10 cm height) with 10 cm spacing on ridges. Water immediately after transplanting. Use shade nets in hot sun.

Week 2: Establishment

Light irrigation every 3–4 days. Spray diluted vermiwash and cover base with mulch to conserve moisture. Monitor for wilting and root health.

Week 3–5: Early Bulbing

Plants enter vegetative and bulbing phase. Apply jeevamrutham through irrigation. Weeding and earthing-up as needed. Monitor for thrips and mites.

Week 6–7: Bulb Swelling

Ensure regular irrigation (not waterlogged). Apply panchagavya foliar spray (3%) every 10 days. Use neem oil (2%) to deter sucking pests.

Week 8–10: Bulb Maturity

Leaves start yellowing. Reduce irrigation to harden bulbs. Apply banana pseudostem extract. Protect against fungal rot using Trichoderma drench.

Week 11–12: Harvest

Harvest when 60–70% tops fall. Pull bulbs and dry in partial sun for 5–7 days. Cut tops and cure fully before storage or market.

  • Rotate with green manure crops to maintain soil fertility.
  • Use wood ash or ash + cow urine mixture to deter fungal rot.
  • Avoid excess irrigation during bulb formation to prevent splitting.
  • Dry harvest under shade to improve storability and avoid fungal attack.
  • Use fermented rice water + jaggery spray as a potassium-rich foliar feed.
