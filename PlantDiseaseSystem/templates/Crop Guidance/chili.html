🌶️ Chili Growing Guide

A Step-by-Step Organic Cultivation Plan

Week -5 to -3: Seed Selection & Nursery

Choose high-yielding, disease-resistant varieties like K1 or CO-1 suited for Madurai. Treat seeds with Trichoderma and soak in Panchagavya before sowing. Raise seedlings in cocopeat or well-drained beds enriched with compost.

Week -2 to 0: Land Preparation

Plough deeply and apply 8–10 tons of well-decomposed FYM/acre. Add 250 kg neem cake + biofertilizers like Azospirillum and Phosphobacteria. Create raised beds or ridges for transplantation.

Week 1: Transplanting

Transplant 30–35 day old seedlings with 45 cm x 45 cm spacing. Water gently. Shade newly transplanted seedlings using banana leaves or shade nets for 3–5 days.

Week 2–3: Rooting Phase

Apply Jeevamrutham and mulch around base to conserve moisture. Light irrigation every 3–4 days. Monitor for damping-off or wilt. Use Trichoderma if needed.

Week 4–5: Canopy Building

Plants start branching. Spray Panchagavya (3%) to enhance leaf growth. Apply compost tea or fermented buttermilk for vitality. First weeding is done.

Week 6–7: Bud Formation

Pinch early buds to encourage branching (optional). Apply vermiwash or cow dung slurry. Keep soil moist but not waterlogged. Set up yellow sticky traps.

Week 8–10: Flowering Begins

Flowers emerge in clusters. Apply banana pseudostem extract and seaweed spray (if available). Keep pests like thrips, mites, and borers under control using neem oil spray (2%).

Week 11–12: Pod Formation

Chilies begin forming. Ensure consistent watering. Apply phosphocompost and buttermilk spray to strengthen pods and reduce blossom drop.

Week 13–16: First Harvest

Start harvesting when pods are firm and green or just turning red (based on market preference). Pick every 4–5 days to encourage new flush.

Week 17–18: Peak Production

Plants give maximum yield. Apply diluted panchagavya and mulch again to retain soil moisture. Control aphids organically. Harvest regularly for uniform pod size.

  • Rotate with legumes or onions to reduce soil-borne diseases.
  • Use cow urine-based fermented sprays to prevent sucking pests.
  • Install pheromone traps for fruit borer monitoring.
  • Do not over-irrigate during flowering to avoid flower drop.
  • Allow 5–6 flushes of harvest before uprooting.
  • Dry red chilies in shade to preserve color and pungency.
