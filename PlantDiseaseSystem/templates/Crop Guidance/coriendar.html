🌿 Coriander Growing Guide

Organic & Seasonal Cultivation Practices

Soil Prep

Choose well-drained loamy soil with neutral pH. Add 2 tons of compost per acre. Plough and level the field well. Add wood ash and neem cake for pest resistance.

Sowing

Soak split coriander seeds for 12 hours before sowing. Sow seeds in rows 20 cm apart. Maintain 10 cm spacing between plants. Lightly cover with soil or compost.

Moisture Management

Water gently every 2 days. Use mulching with dry leaves or straw. Avoid water stagnation. Ideal germination in 7–10 days.

Organic Boosters

Apply Jeevamrutham (1:10 dilution) weekly. Spray Panchagavya 3% to enhance foliage growth and prevent damping-off disease.

Thinning & Weeding

Thin plants to ensure proper spacing (10–12 cm). Perform hand weeding every 10 days. Use neem leaf decoction spray to deter pests.

Growth Boosters

Apply vermiwash (1:5) as foliar spray once in 10 days. Use buttermilk spray for mild fungal resistance and leaf luster.

For leaf harvest, start cutting from 30–35 days onward. Use sharp blade or scissor. Avoid uprooting entire plant for continued regrowth. For seeds, allow full flowering and harvest dry umbels after 90–100 days. Dry thoroughly and store in airtight containers.

  • Use onion/garlic decoction as anti-fungal foliar spray.
  • Intercrop with carrot or spinach for soil balance.
  • Avoid chemical urea – coriander responds best to compost-based nutrition.
  • Sprinkle bioenzyme (citrus peel-based) for leaf freshness before harvest.
  • Use cow urine + asafoetida decoction for aphid control (early morning spray).
