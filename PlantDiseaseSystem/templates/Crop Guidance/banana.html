Banana Growing Guide

1. Choose the Right Variety

Pick healthy banana plants (suckers or tissue culture) of good variety like Grand Naine, Robusta, or Rasthali. Use only disease-free planting material.

2. Prepare the Land

Banana grows well in warm and sunny places. Soil should be rich and well-drained. Dig pits about 2 feet wide and 2 feet deep. Mix topsoil with compost or cow dung.

3. Planting

Plant one healthy sucker in each pit. Keep about 6–8 feet distance between plants. Water immediately after planting.

4. Watering

Bananas need regular water. Keep soil always moist, but not flooded. Water more often in dry season. Use drip irrigation if possible.

5. Manure and Fertilizer

Add compost or farmyard manure every 2–3 months. Use NPK fertilizer in small amounts every month for better growth and yield.

6. Removing Extra Suckers

Keep only 1 or 2 strong suckers near each plant. Remove all other small or weak ones. This helps the main plant to grow strong and give more fruits.

7. Pest and Disease Control

Watch for banana weevil and leaf spot disease. Remove old leaves and keep the field clean. Use neem spray or approved pesticides if needed.

8. Supporting the Plant

Use sticks or bamboo to support plants when bunches become heavy. This prevents the plant from falling down.

9. Harvesting

Bananas are ready in about 10–12 months. Cut bunches when fruits are full size and slightly light green. Handle carefully to avoid damage.

10. After Harvest

Cut down the old plant after harvest and allow the next sucker to grow. Clean the area and add compost to prepare for the next crop.

Follow these simple steps for healthy banana plants and good yield!
