🌽 Corn Growing Guide

A Step-by-Step Organic Cultivation Guide

Week -3 to -2: Soil Analysis

Test soil pH (ideal 5.8–7.0). Add lime if pH is too low. Corn prefers loamy, well-drained soil. Avoid compacted or waterlogged fields.

Week -1: Field Prep

Plough deeply and apply 5–8 tons of well-rotted compost or FYM per acre. Create flat beds or ridges for easy irrigation. Level field for uniform seed depth.

Week 1: Seed Selection & Sowing

Choose region-suited hybrid/double-cross varieties like COH(M) 5 or COH(M) 6. Treat seeds with Trichoderma and Azospirillum. Sow with 60x20 cm spacing at 3-5 cm depth.

Week 2: Germination Monitoring

Ensure good moisture and soil contact. Light irrigation helps sprouting. Watch for fungal damping-off; treat with neem extract if needed.

Week 3–4: Establishment

Apply Panchagavya or jeevamrutham. Irrigate every 5–7 days. Begin weeding. Top-dress with compost tea if leaves appear yellowish.

Week 5–6: Rapid Growth

Plants grow vigorously now. Hill soil around base for stability. Apply liquid biofertilizer (Azotobacter/Phosphobacteria) for nutrient boost.

Week 7: Tasseling

Male flowers (tassels) emerge. Maintain even irrigation. Avoid water stress. Apply banana or cow dung slurry to boost micronutrients.

Week 8–9: Silking

Female silks appear. Pollination occurs now. Ensure good airflow. Use neem-based sprays if aphids or borers are seen.

Week 10–12: Cob Filling

Kernels fill rapidly. Maintain moderate irrigation. Apply potash-rich liquid fertilizer like banana pseudostem extract.

Week 13–14: Maturity & Harvest

Cobs mature when husks turn brown and kernels harden. Harvest early morning or late evening. Dry cobs well before storage.

  • Choose early-maturing varieties for summer and monsoon seasons in Madurai.
  • Use border crops like sunflower or marigold to deter pests.
  • Intercrop with legumes (cowpea) to enhance soil nitrogen.
  • Apply neem cake and Trichoderma viride during land prep.
  • Use yellow sticky traps for aphids and borers.
  • Regular use of jeevamrutham improves plant immunity and yield.
  • Rotate crops with pulses or vegetables to reduce soil fatigue.
