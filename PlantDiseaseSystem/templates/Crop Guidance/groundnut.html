🌱 Groundnut Growing Guide

Your Week-by-Week Organic Journey

Week -4 to -2: Field Planning

Select well-drained sandy loam soil. Perform soil testing and adjust pH if needed (ideal: 6.0–7.5). Avoid waterlogging areas.

Week -2 to 0: Land Preparation

Plough field 2–3 times. Add well-decomposed compost or FYM. Prepare fine tilth with proper ridges and furrows. Ensure good drainage.

Week 1: Sowing

Use bold, disease-free seeds. Inoculate seeds with Rhizobium culture. Sow at 30x10 cm spacing at 4–5 cm depth. Opt for varieties like TMV 7 or VRI 2 for Madurai.

Week 2–3: Germination & Weed Control

Maintain soil moisture. First hand weeding at 15 days. Mulch lightly with straw or leaves if high sun exposure.

Week 4–5: Vigorous Growth

Top dress with compost or vermicompost. Maintain light irrigation. Monitor for leaf spot or rust.

Week 6–8: Pegging Begins

Pegging begins as stems bend toward soil. Avoid disturbing soil. Ensure gentle irrigation and good aeration.

Week 9–12: Pod Maturity

Stop nitrogen inputs. Water lightly. Pods grow underground — check 1-2 for maturity signs like firm shells and brown markings.

Week 13–14: Harvest

Harvest when 75–80% pods are mature. Uproot gently. Dry under shade. Separate pods after drying for 4–5 days.

  • Use Rhizobium and phosphorus-solubilizing bacteria for root health.
  • Apply neem cake and Trichoderma to prevent soilborne disease.
  • Rotate with legumes or cereals to avoid pest buildup.
  • For pest control: Use neem oil spray or cow urine extract every 10 days.
  • Encourage native pollinators with flower borders.
  • Mulch after pegging to conserve moisture and reduce weed growth.
